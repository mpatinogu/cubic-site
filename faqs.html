FAQs

1. What is a whitelist?

Let’s think of it as the list of people that will have exclusive access to an event. Here, the event is the presale where the Cubes of a particular artwork will be launched at a preferential price and only those who secured a spot in the whitelist will be able to buy. The first step in this journey is to register in the whitelist.

2. How should I pay?
You have 2 options:
  • Pay with crypto: You can sign up for a wallet (if you don't already have one) at metamask.io. Then, you need to buy the amount of Ether (ETH) you need to buy the cubes you want (you can do all this through the metamask app, don't worry). Whenever you have your wallet ready, you will be able to buy your cubes directly at our partner marketplace (opensea.io).
  • Pay with card: Through our other partner marketplace (orphic.co) you will be able to use your credit/debit card to buy the cubes you want.
3. I've already registered to the whitelist. Now, where should I buy my Cube?

You will receive an e-mail reminding you of the start of the presale, with a link to access to our platform. There you will be able to select how to pay (crypto or credit/debit card) and then, we will redirect you to one of our 2 partner marketplaces (OpenSea and Orphic) where you will be able to select the number of Cubes you want and pay.

4. What are the main benefits of buying one or more Cubes?
Buying a Cube is like buying a stock. In this case, instead of buying the upside potential of a public company, you will be buying the upside potential of a masterpiece. What does this means? that you will own a fraction of the physical artwork, thus making you the beneficiary of all the value appreciation that the artwork will have (the ArtPrice100 index outperformed 2.5 times the S&P 500 index, which means that art as an investment has yielded, on average, better profits than the stock market). Now, besides making money with it, your Cube will make you a part of the Cubic community and giving you access to exclusive experiences, events, and benefits. So, in other words, is investing in a power asset while having a lot of fun.
5. Is there any pre-requisite such as having a specific account other than my whitelist registration?
Not really. Only if you want to pay with crypto (which we encourage you to do it), you will need to set up a wallet as explained in question number 1.
6. Will somebody be able to buy the entire artwork later in the future at a higher price?
No. The artwork will forever be owned by the Cubic community and will be part of the collection shown at the Cubic Museum, the first decentralized museum in the world. If a Cube holder wants to cash out and enjoy the profits, he/she will be able to sell the Cube at any point giving access to a new person to the Cubic community.
7. For this to be a good investment, should I buy many Cubes?

Not at all. Just as the stock market, the amount you earn will be based on the amount you invested, so if you want to buy 1 Cube or 500 Cubes the profit margin will be the same. Now, we encourage you to buy as many Cubes as possible because this will increase the chances of getting a rare one, which will later have a greater value in the secondary market.

8. What does a rare Cube means?

The Cubes are real, specific fractions of the artwork. You can get one located in the center of the artwork displaying the mouth of the character, or maybe get one in the bottom corner where the artist´s signature is located. These will be “value multipliers” because out of hundreds of Cubes probably only 2 or 3 collectors will be lucky enough to have the artist's signature, for example.

9. What is the difference between investing in physical art and investing in Cubic?

The financial benefits are the same. If the artwork´s value goes up, whenever you sell you will make a profit. The main differences would be that when investing in physical art you will have the added value of having the artwork displayed at home, but you will also be obliged to buy the entire piece, paying a lot of money and losing liquidity. Also, whenever you want to cash out, you will need to find one person to buy the entire artwork which is not going to happen fast, while with Cubic you will be able to either sell all your Cubes or sell only some of your Cubes to have a nice pay day while keeping skin in the game and not losing access to the community.

10. Will there be a physical or virtual museum?

Yes, both. Our goal is to build the Cubic Museum, the first ever decentralized museum in the world, where all the artworks are owned by the community. Also, we will develop the Cubic Virtual Museum, a space in the metaverse to show the entire Cubic Collection.

11. How often will there be any presales or new artworks?

We are planning to launch a new artwork every 2 months or less. For each artwork, there will be a whitelist, a presale and a public sale (only if there are still available Cubes from the presale).

12. How am I supposed to get the return on my investment?

While being the owner of a Cube, you don't need to do anything but wait and see the value going up. The moment you decide to sell one or all your Cubes is the moment you will get the invested capital plus the generated profit. For example, let's say you bought at 55usd per Cube and you decide to sell some months later when each Cube is worth 75usd. You will be getting back your 55usd plus a 20usd profit per each Cube. Also, depending on the rarity of your Cube and the demand for it, the price can go higher than the actual value of the artwork.
